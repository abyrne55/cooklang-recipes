# Vegan Chocolate Chip Cookies

Yields ~30 cookies

## Ingredients

**Tip:** Measure by weight (i.e., ditch the measuring spoons for a kitchen scale) for best results.

* 2 tbsp (16 g) flaxseed meal
* 6 tbsp (90 ml) warm water
* 2¾ cups (330 g) all-purpose flour
* ¼ cup (30 g) bread flour
* 1 tsp (5 g) baking soda
* ½ tsp (3 g) baking powder
* 2 tsp (5 g) cornstarch
* ½ tsp (3 g) fine sea salt
* 1 tsp (2 g) ground cinnamon
* 1 cup + 2 tbsp (240 g) vegan butter, softened (65–68°F)
* 1 cup minus 1 tbsp (190 g) granulated sugar (turbinado/raw sugar works well)
* 1 cup + 2 tbsp (230 g) light brown sugar, packed
* 2 tsp (10 g) vanilla extract
* 2 cups (340 g) dairy-free chocolate chips

## Steps

1. In a small bowl, whisk the flaxseed meal with the warm water. Let sit until thick and gloopy, at least 10 minutes.

2. In a medium bowl, whisk together both flours, baking soda, baking powder, cornstarch, salt, and cinnamon.

3. Using a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, cream the butter and both sugars on medium speed until light and fluffy, about 2 minutes.

4. On low speed, add the prepared flaxseed mixture, then the vanilla extract. Mix for about 1 minute, until smooth. It’s okay if the mixture looks slightly “broken” at this point.

5. On low speed, add the dry ingredients and mix until just incorporated. Do not overmix.

6. Fold in the chocolate chips until evenly distributed. Do not overmix.

7. Portion the dough into about thirty 50 g balls, and wrap each dough ball in a small piece of plastic wrap (see note).

8. Refrigerate the wrapped dough balls to allow the flavors to meld and the flax to fully hydrate, helping bind the dough.

9. After 24–72 hours of refrigeration, and at least 2 hours before baking, transfer the dough balls to the freezer. Frozen dough will spread less than refrigerated dough in the oven.

10. About 30 minutes before baking, preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line two large (half-sheet) pans and one medium (quarter-sheet) pan with parchment paper. Keep the pans away from the oven so they don’t warm up prematurely.

11. Working quickly, unwrap and place the frozen dough balls on the prepared pans, leaving about 2 inches between each cookie (~12 cookies per large sheet, ~6 per medium).

12. Bake from frozen for 12–14 minutes, until the edges are lightly golden and the centers look slightly underdone. The cookies will seem soft straight from the oven but will firm up as they cool.

13. Remove the pans from the oven and place them on the stovetop. Let the cookies rest on the pans for 5 minutes (they’re still cooking), then transfer to a wire rack using a spatula.

14. Let cool until sturdy enough to handle without crumbling (10–15 minutes), then enjoy with a glass of non-dairy milk.

Note: to reduce plastic waste, you can put dough balls in airtight freezer-safe containers (e.g., lidded Pyrex) instead of plastic-wrapping them. The important thing is that the cookie dough balls aren't touching and won't dry out
